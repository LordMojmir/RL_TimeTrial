# Trial Run
## Game Concept (2–3 sentences)

Monaco Time Trial is a top-down racing time-trial game inspired by the tight streets of Monaco. The player controls a single car and tries to complete laps as fast as possible while avoiding collisions with barriers. 

## Key Gameplay Mechanics (2–3 sentences each)
1) Car Control & Physics
The player controls acceleration, braking, and steering to drive the car through the track. The car has momentum, meaning it won’t stop instantly and will slide slightly during sharp turns. Good lap times depend on managing speed and direction smoothly.

2) Collision & Track Limits
The track is bounded by barriers. If the car hits a wall, it either slows heavily or resets to a nearby safe position (depending on the angel). This punishes reckless driving and rewards clean racing lines.

3) Lap Timing & Progress Tracking
A timer tracks lap time from start to start line. The game records a best lap time and displays it on-screen so the player can try to beat it.

## What Will Make It Fun (1–2 paragraphs)
This form of game has worked before and has been inspired by Trackmania and simular time-trial racing games. The fun comes from mastering the car and improving lap by lap, and optionally letting your friends drive and compare with them.

The setting also creates tension: Monaco-style tracks feels intimate  because there’s little room for error. Even with simple graphics, tight corners and barriers create exciting moments. Over time, players learn the track as well as the controls, and feel real skill growth.

## Storyline / Theme / Setting

The game is set on a street circuit inspired by Monaco's narrow roads, sharp turns, and barriers. There is no complex story; the theme is professional time-trial racing and precision driving. The setting supports gameplay by making speed risky and difficult to controle.

## Art and Audio

Visually, the game will use a clean top-down style: a dark road surface, lighter borders/barriers, and a distinct colored car. Simple UI text displays current time and best time. No Audio.
